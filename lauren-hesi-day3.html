Today's goal: Know exactly which organ does what — don't mix them up! Pay special attention to liver vs. gallbladder.
⚠️ Most Confused Pair

⚡ Liver vs. Gallbladder — Know the difference cold

🫀 Liver
PRODUCES bile
The liver is the factory. It manufactures bile continuously and sends it through the hepatic duct toward the small intestine.
VS
💚 Gallbladder
STORES bile
The gallbladder is the warehouse. It concentrates and stores bile until fatty food enters the duodenum — then it releases it.
Memory rule: L for Liver = L for Lab (makes it)  |  G for Gallbladder = G for Garage (stores it)
🔬 Bile & Fat Digestion
🧪 What is Bile?
  • A green-yellow digestive fluid
  • Made of bile salts, cholesterol, bilirubin, water
  • Produced by hepatocytes (liver cells)
  • NOT an enzyme — it's an emulsifier
💧 What Does Bile Do?
  • Emulsifies fats → breaks big fat globules into tiny droplets
  • Increases surface area for lipase (enzyme) to work
  • Released into the duodenum (first part of small intestine)
  • Essential for fat-soluble vitamin absorption (A, D, E, K)
🔁 Fat Digestion Path
  • Fat enters duodenum → CCK hormone released
  • CCK signals gallbladder to contract
  • Bile released → emulsifies fat
  • Pancreatic lipase finishes breaking fat into fatty acids
🫁 Key Organ Functions
👄 Mouth

Mechanical + Chemical Start

  • Teeth chew food (mechanical digestion)
  • Salivary amylase breaks down starches
  • Tongue forms food into a bolus
  • First place food enters the system
🔽 Esophagus

Transport Only

  • Carries food from mouth to stomach via peristalsis
  • No digestion occurs here
  • Lower esophageal sphincter prevents acid reflux
🫙 Stomach

Protein Digestion Begins

  • Churns food → chyme (semi-liquid)
  • HCl (hydrochloric acid) kills bacteria, activates pepsin
  • Pepsin breaks down proteins
  • Intrinsic factor made here (needed for B12 absorption)
〰️ Small Intestine

Main Absorption Site

  • Duodenum: bile + pancreatic enzymes arrive here
  • Jejunum: major nutrient absorption
  • Ileum: vitamin B12 + bile salt reabsorption
  • Villi & microvilli maximize surface area
🔚 Large Intestine

Water & Electrolytes

  • Absorbs water and electrolytes from waste
  • Forms and stores feces
  • Houses gut bacteria (microbiome)
  • No significant nutrient digestion
🫀 Liver

The Multi-Tasker

  • Produces bile (sent to small intestine)
  • Metabolizes carbs, proteins, fats
  • Detoxifies drugs and alcohol
  • Produces clotting factors and albumin
  • Stores glycogen, vitamins A/D/B12
💚 Gallbladder

Bile Storage & Release

  • Stores and concentrates bile from the liver
  • Releases bile into duodenum when fat is detected
  • CCK hormone triggers its contraction
  • Can be removed (cholecystectomy) without stopping digestion
🦠 Pancreas

Enzymes + Hormones

  • Exocrine: releases amylase, lipase, protease into duodenum
  • Endocrine: produces insulin and glucagon (blood sugar)
  • Releases bicarbonate to neutralize stomach acid
  • Key HESI distinction: dual function (exo + endocrine)
📍 Food's Journey — Step by Step
1

Mouth

Chewing + salivary amylase begin carbohydrate breakdown. Bolus formed, swallowed.

2

Esophagus

Peristalsis moves bolus down. No digestion. Sphincter opens into stomach.

3

Stomach

HCl + pepsin attack proteins. Churning produces chyme. Controlled release into duodenum.

4

Small Intestine (Duodenum → Jejunum → Ileum)

Bile from gallbladder emulsifies fats. Pancreatic enzymes complete digestion. Nutrients absorbed through villi.

5

Large Intestine

Water and electrolytes absorbed from remaining waste. Feces formed and stored.

6

Rectum & Anus

Waste eliminated from the body.
