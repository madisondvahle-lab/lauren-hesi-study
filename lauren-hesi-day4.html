🫘 The Four Structures
🫘
Kidneys

Filter & Producer

  • Bean-shaped organs — one on each side of the spine
  • Filter all of the blood roughly every 30 minutes
  • Remove waste products (urea, creatinine) from blood
  • Produce urine as the byproduct of filtration
  • Also regulate water balance, blood pressure, and electrolytes
  • Contain the nephron — the functional filtering unit
〰️
Ureters

The Delivery Tubes

  • Two narrow muscular tubes (one per kidney)
  • Carry urine from the kidney down to the bladder
  • Use peristalsis (rhythmic muscle squeezes) to move urine
  • About 25–30 cm long in adults
  • Connect at the renal pelvis (the kidney's "funnel")
  • Do not store or filter — transport only
🫧
Bladder

The Storage Tank

  • Hollow muscular organ in the lower pelvis
  • Stores urine until the body is ready to void (urinate)
  • Lined with transitional epithelium (stretches as it fills)
  • Holds ~400–600 mL in a healthy adult
  • Stretch receptors signal the brain when it's time to void
  • The detrusor muscle contracts during urination
🔽
Urethra

The Exit Route

  • Short tube that carries urine from the bladder out of the body
  • Female urethra: ~4 cm (shorter → higher UTI risk)
  • Male urethra: ~20 cm (longer, also carries semen)
  • Two sphincters control urine flow: internal (involuntary) & external (voluntary)
  • Final stop in the urinary pathway
⚗️ How Urine Is Made (in the Nephron)

The 3-Step Process Inside Each Kidney

1
Filtration

Blood is pushed under pressure through tiny capillaries in the glomerulus. Water, salts, glucose, amino acids, and wastes pass into the nephron tubule. Large proteins and blood cells stay in the blood — they're too big to cross.

2
Reabsorption

As the filtered fluid travels through the tubule, the body reclaims what it needs — most water, glucose, and essential salts are pulled back into the bloodstream. This keeps you from losing valuable nutrients in urine.

3
Secretion

Additional waste products (like hydrogen ions and certain drugs) are actively pushed out of the blood into the tubule fluid. This fine-tunes the composition of urine and helps maintain blood pH.

📌 HESI Must-Know Facts

🔑 Key Facts

Urine direction: Kidneys → Ureters → Bladder → Urethra
Kidney job: Filter blood AND produce urine
Ureter job: Transport only — no storage, no filtration
Bladder job: Storage — the only urinary organ that holds urine
Urethra job: Final exit — carries urine out of the body
Female urethra is shorter → higher UTI risk
Peristalsis moves urine through the ureters
Nephron is the kidney's functional filtering unit
3 steps: Filtration → Reabsorption → Secretion
Detrusor muscle in bladder wall contracts to void
