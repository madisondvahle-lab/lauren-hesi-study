Key Vocabulary Words

10 terms
Benign
Not cancerous; not harmful or progressive. Used to describe tumors that do not invade surrounding tissue.
e.g., "The biopsy confirmed the mass was benign."
Malignant
Cancerous; tending to spread, invade, and destroy surrounding tissue. Opposite of benign.
e.g., "The pathology report indicated a malignant tumor."
Prognosis
A forecast of the probable course and outcome of a disease or condition.
e.g., "The patient's prognosis is favorable with treatment."
Adverse
Harmful or unfavorable. Often used with "effect" to describe unexpected negative reactions to a drug.
e.g., "Nausea is an adverse effect of the medication."
Chronic
Persisting for a long time (3+ months), often progressing slowly. Contrast with acute.
e.g., "She has chronic lower back pain."
Acute
Sudden onset, severe in nature, and short in duration. Requires prompt medical attention.
e.g., "He was admitted with acute appendicitis."
Anterior
Situated at or toward the front of the body. Also called "ventral" in anatomy.
e.g., "The anterior cruciate ligament (ACL) is at the front of the knee."
Posterior
Situated at or toward the back of the body. Opposite of anterior.
e.g., "The posterior tibial artery runs along the back of the lower leg."
Diagnosis
The process of identifying a disease or condition by examining symptoms, test results, and history.
e.g., "The diagnosis of diabetes was confirmed by a blood test."
Symptom
A subjective sign of disease felt by the patient (pain, fatigue) but not directly measurable by others.
e.g., "Headache and dizziness were her primary symptoms."
💡 Memory Tip: Pair opposites! Benign vs. Malignant, Acute vs. Chronic, Anterior vs. Posterior. Learning them as contrast pairs is proven to double retention.

Common Medical Prefixes

6 prefixes
Prefix Meaning HESI Examples
brady- Slow bradycardia (slow heart rate), bradypnea (slow breathing)
tachy- Fast, rapid tachycardia (fast heart rate), tachypnea (rapid breathing)
hypo- Below normal, deficient hypoglycemia (low blood sugar), hypotension (low blood pressure)
hyper- Above normal, excessive hypertension (high blood pressure), hyperglycemia (high blood sugar)
anti- Against, opposing antibiotic (against bacteria), anticoagulant (against clotting)
sub- Under, below subcutaneous (under the skin), sublingual (under the tongue)
💡 Pattern Trick: brady/tachy and hypo/hyper are both fast/slow and low/high pairs. If you see bradycardia on the HESI, your patient has a slow heart — think "Brady is slow to the party."

Common Medical Suffixes

5 suffixes
Suffix Meaning HESI Examples
-itis Inflammation of appendicitis, bronchitis, arthritis, tonsillitis
-ectomy Surgical removal appendectomy, tonsillectomy, mastectomy, hysterectomy
-ology Study of cardiology (heart), neurology (nerves), oncology (cancer)
-oscopy Visual examination (using a scope) colonoscopy, bronchoscopy, arthroscopy
-plasty Surgical repair or reconstruction rhinoplasty (nose), angioplasty (blood vessel), arthroplasty (joint)
💡 Quick Decode: "Appendicitis" = inflammation of the appendix. "Appendicectomy" = removal of the appendix. Swap the suffix, change the meaning. This decoding skill alone can answer dozens of HESI questions!
🧩

Putting It Together — Word Building

Medical words are built like puzzles. Most have a root (body part or concept), an optional prefix (before the root), and an optional suffix (after the root). Once you know the parts, you can decode words you've never seen.

brady + cardia = bradycardia — slow + heart = slow heart rate
hyper + glyc + emia = hyperglycemia — high + sugar + blood condition = high blood sugar
gastr + itis = gastritis — stomach + inflammation = inflammation of the stomach
sub + cutane + ous = subcutaneous — under + skin + pertaining to = pertaining to under the skin
